团队情况

Storms & TerrAin Research (STAR) Group at the Institute of Atmospheric Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences (IAP/CAS)

Our research group focuses on advancing the understanding of severe convective weather and extreme precipitation events across China. We investigate the preconvective environments, mesoscale convective systems (MCSs), and mesoscale vortices that drive hazardous winds, thunderstorms, and torrential rainfall. By combining climatological analyses, case studies of landmark events, and high-resolution numerical simulations, we reveal the physical mechanisms behind storm initiation, organization, and evolution.

A distinctive feature of our work is the emphasis on terrain–atmosphere interactions, such as the role of the second-step terrain (SST), mountain basins, and regional circulation patterns in modulating convection and rainfall distribution. We also explore moisture transport pathways, low-level jets, and synoptic-scale modulations that sustain long-lived convective systems.

Beyond fundamental science, our team contributes to forecasting and nowcasting innovations, developing enhanced storm warning models, probability-based rainfall prediction methods, and evaluating satellite and reanalysis datasets for operational applications. Together, these efforts aim to improve disaster mitigation strategies and strengthen resilience against high-impact weather in China and beyond.
